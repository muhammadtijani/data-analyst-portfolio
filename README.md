# data-analyst-portfolio
# Brazilian E-Commerce Analysis
Goal: Find out why customers stop shopping and which products make the most money.

Tools I Used
 SQL: To join different tables together.
 Python: To clean the data and make charts.
 Tableau/Power BI: To create a final dashboard.

 The Story So Far
I am currently analyzing 100,000 orders from 2016 to 2018. 
I want to answer: "Does slow shipping make people quit the app?"

# Key Finding: The "Retention Gap"
After analyzing 96,000+ unique customers, I identified a significant retention challenge:
Churn Statistic:72.8% of customers are at "High Risk" of churning (no purchase in 180+ days).
Observation: The distribution shows a massive "Long Tail" of one-time shoppers who did not return after their first experience, indicating a lack of post-purchase engagement.

Business Recommendations
Based on these findings, I recommend the following three actions for the Marketing and Operations teams:

1. Implement a "Win-Back" Campaign: Create an automated email sequence offering a 15% "We Miss You" discount specifically for customers entering the 180-day dormant window.
2. Launch a Loyalty Tier: Since the majority of users are one-time buyers, introducing a "Second Purchase Bonus" (e.g., free shipping on the 2nd order) could significantly increase the Customer Lifetime Value (CLV).
3. Logistics Deep-Dive: My analysis suggested a correlation between shipping delays and churn. The business should audit logistics partners in regions where "Estimated vs. Actual" delivery dates vary by more than 3 days.
